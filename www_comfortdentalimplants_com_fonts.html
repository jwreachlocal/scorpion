San Jose Dental Implant Dentist

Dental Implants in San Jose, Santa Clara, Los Gatos & Campbell, CA

Are you ashamed of your smile because you have missing or damaged teeth? Dental implants are a simple solution to replace teeth that have been broken, knocked out, or fallen out, and may permanently repair your beautiful smile. They allow you to eat as you did prior to losing your teeth, and may help you regain a new sense of confidence. There are several different types of dental implants, and depending on the person, an individual may have multiple smile-restoring options.

Dental implants are becoming more and more popular because they replicate the natural function and appearance of missing teeth. When a person undergoes a dental implant, they are having a small, sturdy titanium post that acts as a natural tooth's root structure permanently placed either in or on their jaw. Once the bone grows around the implant, they may hold a crown, just as roots hold natural teeth in place. If you are interested in dental implants, please contact the dental implant dentist in San Jose at Comfort Dental. We also proudly serve patients in Santa Clara, Los Gatos, and Campbell.

Why get dental implants?

Dental implants are a permanent way to repair your smile after you have lost teeth due to periodontal disease or injury. There are two types of dental implants—endosteal and subperiosteal implants. Endosteal implants are the most common type and are often used for patients who have bridges or removable dentures. Screws, cylinders, or blades are surgically implanted into the jaw bone, and each implant holds one or more prosthetic teeth. Subperiosteal implants are placed on top of the jaw with metal posts protruding through the gum to hold the prosthesis. People who are unable to wear dentures and have minimal jaw bone height may find this type of dental implant ideal. For further information, read our dental implants FAQ page.

Dental Implant Qualifications

A person who is in good general and oral health is idyllic for the dental implant procedure. They must have enough bone in their jaw to support the implant, and the best candidate is someone who has healthy gum tissues. Implants are connected with the gum tissues and jaw bone, which is why it is crucial for an implant recipient to have healthy teeth and healthy gums. The qualified periodontists at Comfort Dental will ensure that a person is healthy enough to receive dental implants prior to surgery.

Once we deem a patient fit to receive implants, our dentists will determine which type of dental implant is best for the patient and their smile. After they have successfully received dental implants, our team will develop a personalized care plan for their implants, and several follow-up visits may be scheduled to monitor their implants, other teeth, and gums to make sure that everything is healthy and fully-functioning.
